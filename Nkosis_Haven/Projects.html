Key Projects at Nkosi's Haven

Nkosi's Haven Village (Alan Manor)

Their main focus of the organisation activities is the Nkosi's Haven Village located in Johannesburg, Alan Manor which was created in 2002. With the assistance of the Gauteng Depaerment of Housing, the village was created and it has now turned into a place of empowerment, education and most importantly safety. The village aims to encourage a community there the residents heal and regain their dignity while being provided a sanctuary

    The Village includes 17 residential cottages where families live together, supported by facilities such as:
  • Sickbay and Therapy block
  • Classrooms and Library
  • Baby daycare and Workshops
  • Music and arts centre and Leisure spaces
  • Industrial kitchen
  • Preschool which is still in developement

Nkosi’s 4Life Farm

In 2008, Nkosi’s Haven created Nkosi’s 4Life Farm in Johannesburg to add onto their vision of sustainability. It was made so that the Village would be able to get food supply and the women could go work on the farms and if there are extra crops, they could sell the extra crops to genereate income for themselves. This teaches the women how to become independent and learn how to work for themselves.

    These are the objectives of the Nkosi's Haven 4Life Farm:
  • Crops feed the residents and other communities around
  • Receive profit by seeling some of the produce
  • Help people in need affected by HIV/AIDS

Nkosi’s Haven Preschool Development

The Nkosi’s Haven Preschool is a vital new initiative currently under development within Nkosi’s Haven Village in Johannesburg. This project is investing in the long term developement of the children that they care for. It emphasizes the belief that every child deserves a good and strong start in life regardless of their backgrounds or health status

    Objectives of the Preschool:
  • Provide Early Childhood Education: Children from 2–6 must learn about literacy, numeracy, and social skills
  • Support Cognitive and Emotional Development: Learning activities and therapeutic play suitable for the childrens age
  • Create a routine and stability: Children who have experienced trauma or instability can have a space where they can grow freely
  • Enable Early HIV Education and Awareness: Age-appropriate health education for HIV-positive or affected children
  • Empower Mothers Through Participation: Involve and train resident mothers to become teaching assistants or staff
  • Prepare Children for Formal Schooling: Ensure readiness for Grade R and primary school through a strong foundation
