Nkosi Johnson

A brave child who changed the world for children with HIV/AIDS

Nkosi’s Early Life

Nkosi Johnson, born on the 4th of February 1989 was born with HIV. His mother Nonthlanthla Nkosi was someone who had HIV and when Nkosi was 8, she passed away . She was not able to take care of him because of her health so she had no choice but to ask Gail Johnson who was a volunteer at an AIDS care center to take care of Nkosi. He was adopted by Gil legally and his name was changed to Nkosi Johnson.

His Past Expirence

When Nkosi was 8, he expirenced discrimination when a primary school did'nt want to admit him because he had HIV. After that schools had to accept children with HIV/AIDS after a ruling made in court At the age of 8, Nkosi faced discrimination when a primary school refused to admit him due to his HIV status. A court ruling later mandated that schools accept HIV-positive children, challenging widespread stigma. He stressed in a speech he said when he was 11 at the 13th International AIDS Conference that people need to accept children living with HIV/AIDS and also adding that:

“We are normal. We have hands. We have feet. We can walk, we can talk, we have needs just like everyone else—don’t be afraid of us—we are all the same!”(Nkosi Johnson, 2000)

Nkosi’s Haven

Nkosi and Gail founded Nkosi’s Haven because Gail was inspired by the challenges that Nkosi faced and his past experiences. The organisation stays true to Nkosi's goal of a world that does not discriminate or fear families affected by HIV/AIDS. Nkosi has left behind an impact globally even though his life was cut short. He was able to raise awareness about HIV/AIDS while going against discrimination which led to organisations like Nkosi’s Haven and others to be inspired
